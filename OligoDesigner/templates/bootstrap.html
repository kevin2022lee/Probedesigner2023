QuantiMAT技术是一种基于分支链信号扩增技术开发的国产化第二代信号放大技术，OligoDesigner可以根据所选靶标的序列信息，生成各种QuantiMAT实验所需 的各种功能性探针组CE（capture probes）、LE（Label Extender）、BL（Blocker）。这些探针组通过了严苛的NSH（非特异性杂交分析），使得探针和通用2.0序列产生最小的NSH 确保实验的高特异性、高灵敏度。

filter说明

Filtering is a powerful tool for changing the allowable service of probes. Using the filter criteria, you can either decrease or increase the number of probes which are available for use as CEs or LEs in the assay.

The details of filtering are discussed in the section on Filtering in the chapter on “Probeset Design using OligoDesigner” . It should be noted that the primary reason for changing the filter criteria is to increase the number of CEs and LEs that are available for use in the assay. Although the default thresholds are excellent guidelines, they are….just that, guidelines. In many cases, you may have probes that are only slightly above or below the allowable thresholds.

You, as the probeset designer (I’ll bet you’ve never been called that before), will need to review the properties of each of the probes in your probeset to determine which, if any, can be relaxed without negatively affecting assay performance.

Remember, the action of “filtering” (i.e., relaxing the filter criteria) relaxes the filters for all the probes in the tree. You cannot relax filters for individual probes. When you relax a filter for one probe you may change the status of other probes in a favorable manner.

CE-LE interactions说明

CE-LE interactions are the final source of assay background considered by OligoDesigner. In fact, these interactions are used to establish the final assignment of the probes in the assay. Complementary CEs and LEs could interact via the mechanism shown below. This may be prevented by excluding one of the probes from service in the probeset or by assigning the same service to both probes. The process of assigning probes, calculating CE-LE interactions, and reassigning as necessary is, as you can imagine, an iterative process. This process is enabled using the Assignment View.

Similar to CE and LE NSH calculations, the CE-LE interaction is evaluated using the x-mer algorithm. PD searches all possible x-mers that could form between the extenders (CEs vs. LEs), estimates their stability, and sums them. Similarly to the NSH calculations, it applies a weight to the x-mer results to estimate the contribution of the CE-LE hybrid to background. All CE-LE interactions are given a weight of 6 similar to LE-AMP leader interactions for the system 1 assays.

NSH的定义

NSH interactions are interactions involving capture and label extenders and universals which lead to elevated leve ls of background in bDNA assays. Minimizing NSH is an essential part of designing a successful probeset. As such, OligoDesigner screens probes against the universals to exclude those that could lead to elevated background through NSH interactions. (For a published example of NSH interactions, see M. Collins et. al., NAR Vol 25, no. 15, Aug 1997).

Before proceeding, it is important that we define “universal” sequences. These are the molecules, common to all bDNA assays (of the same universal chemistry type), that mediate the capture of the target and the signal amplification. For SYSTEM 1 assays (open kits), they include the branched DNA molecule, the AP probe and the capture probe (PSCP) on the plate. For the SYSTEM 8 assays, they include the branched DNA molecule (with iso-C, iso-G), the pre-amplifier molecule and the capture probe on the plate. The CE and LE tails (also called overhangs) are also considered “universal”; after concatenation with the specific extender sequence via a spacer, they enable the hybridization of the probes to the universals and subsequent signal amplification. For each chemistry, the universal sequences are grouped in a file and imported into OligoDesigner as a set.

    The screening of probes from a probeset that could lead to higher background involves the following:
  • identifying the extender-universal interactions that could interact non-specifically
  • estimating the stability of the hybrids that form between the extenders and the universals
  • estimating the contribution of any potentially stable interaction to background

The NSH interactions for System 1 chemistry are depicted below. The interactions differ depending on whether the probe is a CE or an LE. Early in the design process we haven’t specified probe service; any probe can be either a CE or an LE. Hence, the analysis is performed twice for all probes: once assuming service as a CE and once assuming service as an LE.

OligoDesigner estimates the stability of hybrids and their contribution to background. For each interaction the hybrid stability is evaluated using the x-mer algorithm (discussed below), and the contribution of the hybrid to background is calculated by multiplying this predicted stability by a weighting factor specific to the interaction. The score associated with each interaction for the system 1 universals is shown below. Although the default settings are recommended, the hybrid parameters and the NSH weighting factors can be changed within the program using the NSH Dialog.

As an example, note that the CE-AMP arm interaction has a weight of 15X. If the stability of the hybrid is calculated from the x-mer algorithm to be, say, “1”, the CE NSH score for this interactions would be 15 (the x-mer result times 15). Now, if you are paying attention, you are wondering why the CE-“AMP arm” interaction given more weight than the CE-“AMP leader” and CE-“AP probe” interaction. For the 15X3 molecule, each of the AMP arms has three binding sites for the AP probe. This yields 45 binding sites for both the AP and the CE probe should there be non-specific pairing. Conceptually you can envision why this is an NSH interaction we wish to avoid.
